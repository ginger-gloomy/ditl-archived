Smart Dispenser
  • Reduce filter replacement frequency 
    • Manufacturers change filters in batches 
    • Test water quality while replacing the filter 
    • Change the filter every three months (too much) 
    • Present our results of the analysis to school administrators 
      • Change the contract with the manufacturer
      • Reduce the frequency of replacing the filter and save money for school
      • Provide company water data as a maintenance reference
      • Change control policy 
  • Avoid useless energy consumption 
    • Avoid overheating 
    • Avoid useless power, like no one to use but to heat or cool 
    • Regulations require more than 95 degrees to eliminate E. coli, so in order to avoid the bacteria, the three-cylinder water will all be boiled. 
    • It is the most energy-intensive when entering the water. 
    • It is recommended that the warm water tank should be the largest, because the temperature of the cold and hot water is the most energy-consuming. 
  • Adjustment of power saving mode 
    • Avoid frequent heating and cooling 
    • In the power saving mode, if a user suddenly wants to fill the water, it will be reheated, we can use mobile app to show where water can be used. 
    • We also should let the system know that someone is using the water dispenser when in power saving mode 
    • Avoid letting the user drink the water temperature they want to drink

    •  Find the proper sterilization mode
